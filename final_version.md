# Winston Qiu – AI, Smart Products & Health Tech

## About Me

Winston Qiu is a graduate student in Information Systems and AI at Johns Hopkins University, with a background in Business Analytics and Entrepreneurship. His interest lies in building AI-powered products that are not just technically sound but genuinely useful to everyday people — particularly in domains where data is abundant but insight is scarce.

## Problem Area I Care About

Winston is focused on applying AI to smart consumer health products, with a specific interest in passive, continuous monitoring that doesn't require users to change their behavior. His smart pet toilet project is a direct example: a device that collects biological data from pets during their normal routine, then uses AI to flag early health anomalies — conditions that often go unnoticed until they become serious. The broader vision is a category of ambient health tools that work quietly in the background, for both animals and people.

## Three Ways Generative AI Could Help

- **Plain-language health summaries:** Convert raw sensor readings into clear, owner-friendly explanations — moving beyond alerts to actual context and guidance.
- **Longitudinal pattern recognition:** Build individualized baselines for each animal over time, so deviations are flagged relative to *that pet's* normal, not a population average.
- **Structured vet handoffs:** Auto-generate concise clinical summaries from weeks of monitoring data, giving veterinarians a faster, more complete starting point for consultations.
